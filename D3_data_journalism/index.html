Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

In recent decades, healthcare has become a major concern for the U.S. population, due to high costs and longer life expectancies. A study was commissioned to ascertain the links between variables associated with healthcare and financial well-being. Values were obtained from all 50 states.

The results of the study showed that certain factors correlated well, while others didn't. The following showed correlations, indicating that further studies might be worthwhile.

-Population without Healthcare (%) vs Poverty (%) (Positive Correlation)

-Smokers (%) vs Median Household Income (%) (Negative Correlation)

-Smokers (%) vs Poverty (%) (Negative Correlation)

-Obesity (%) vs Median Household Income (%) (Negative Correlation)

The data would suggest that the way to improve health outcomes (as indicated by the healthcare, smoker, & obesity (%s)) is to improve the financial well-being of a state's populace. However, further studies are needed to strengthen this conclusion.
